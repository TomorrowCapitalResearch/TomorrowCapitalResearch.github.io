Research Framework

Tomorrow Capital Research

At Tomorrow Capital Research, we treat the firm as a single integrated system rather than a set of isolated desks. Our research is organised into four connected layers: Business Strategy, Portfolio Management, Trading Strategies and Execution.

Each layer has a clear job, but they constantly feed information to one another so the entire system can adapt as markets and competitors change.

Overview

Our Four-Layer Framework

Business Strategy

Decide where we compete, when to expand, and how each new venture fits into the long-term plan.

Portfolio Management

Allocate capital and risk across all strategies and asset classes, optimising multiple KPIs at once.

Trading Strategies

Turn research into systematic models that generate concrete buy and sell decisions.

Execution

Interact with markets efficiently and safely, minimising cost, slippage and information leakage.

Layer 1

Business Strategy

The Business Strategy layer acts as the firm’s “executive brain”. It decides which businesses to build, how fast to scale them, and how they fit together across time.

  • Expansion path: crypto & digital assets → sports markets → traditional markets (equities, FX, derivatives), guided by data rather than fashion.
  • Venture evaluation: AI-driven scoring of expected return, risk, liquidity, infrastructure needs and competitive response before committing capital.
  • Firm-level constraints: clear global limits on risk, leverage and drawdown that cascade down to portfolios and strategies.

The goal is simple: profitable, controlled expansion where each new venture strengthens the overall system.

Layer 2

Portfolio Management System

The Portfolio Management layer sits between the business and the trading strategies. It decides how to allocate capital and risk across desks, time horizons and styles.

  • Multi-KPI optimisation: return, volatility, drawdowns, liquidity, concentration and diversification are treated jointly, not in isolation.
  • Dynamic rebalancing: allocations shift as regimes, market conditions and strategy performance change.
  • Portfolio of portfolios: thematic and asset-class sub-portfolios managed by local engines, coordinated by a top-level portfolio brain.

This layer ensures that no single idea dominates the firm, and that capital flows to where it is used best under current conditions.

Layer 3

Trading Strategies

The Trading Strategies layer is where research becomes systematic trading. We maintain a diverse library of algorithms across assets, styles and time scales.

  • Strategy types: market-neutral stat-arb, trend-following, mean-reversion, market-making, event-driven and more, tailored to each market.
  • Methodology: a mix of classical quantitative models and modern machine learning, including reinforcement learning agents where appropriate.
  • Research pipeline: idea → backtest → simulation → paper trading → controlled live deployment with tight risk limits.

Each strategy has clear objectives and constraints from the portfolio layer, and reports detailed performance back up the stack.

Layer 4

Execution & Microstructure

The Execution layer connects our models to real markets. Its job is to achieve the desired trades with minimal cost, slippage and information leakage.

  • Smart routing: venue selection, order type choice and schedule optimisation based on live microstructure conditions.
  • Infrastructure: low-latency gateways, monitoring and redundancy for time-sensitive strategies where the cost is justified.
  • Stealth & safety: market impact controls, throttles, pre-trade checks and kill-switches to prevent abnormal behaviour.

Execution constantly feeds back data on fills, latency and market impact, improving both the strategies and the portfolio management system.

Feedback Loop

One System, Four Layers

From top to bottom, Business Strategy sets direction, Portfolio Management allocates capital, Trading Strategies generate orders, and Execution connects to markets.

From bottom to top, detailed trading and execution data flows back into the system, allowing each layer to learn, adapt and refine its decisions. The research objective is to make this loop faster, more stable and more intelligent over time.
